About Me

I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Robotics at Hanyang University ERICA and a member of the Human–Robot Collaboration Laboratory. My research focuses on developing intelligent robotic systems that can perceive human states, understand collaborative situations, and generate safe and adaptive robot behaviors.

My current research interests include human–robot collaboration, mobile manipulation, human intention and state recognition, socially aware robot navigation, and multimodal robot perception. I am particularly interested in integrating human-centered perception with robot motion planning and control to support proactive, safe, and context-aware collaboration between humans and robots.

I have also conducted research on mobile robot localization and motion control, including sensor-fusion-based indoor three-dimensional localization using ultra-wideband, inertial, and barometric sensors, as well as online kinematic parameter estimation for skid-steering mobile robots.

Research Interests

  • Mobile Manipulation and Collaborative Robot Behavior
